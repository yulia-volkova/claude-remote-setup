# Astra Strategy Stream -- Weekly Exercises

Use these exercises during the quiz stage or wrap-up to deepen engagement with each week's material. These are adapted from the Astra Strategy Stream 4-week curriculum.

## Week 1: Timelines / Takeoff

- Compare at least two takeoff models (e.g., slow vs. fast, continuous vs. discontinuous). What evidence would distinguish them?
- Play with the Epoch AI compute-centric framework web app. Share a forecast for when transformative AI arrives and explain your reasoning.
- Estimate: given current scaling trends, when does training compute reach [X] FLOP? What assumptions does your estimate rest on?
- Steelman and then critique the "intelligence explosion" argument. What is the strongest objection?

## Week 2: Misaligned AI Takeover / Scheming

- Estimate P(scheming) based on the arguments in the paper you just read. Show your reasoning step by step.
- List the key conditions under which deceptive alignment becomes likely. For each condition, assess how plausible it is.
- Compare the "behavioral selection" model of scheming with the "training game" model. Which is more concerning and why?
- If you had to argue that scheming is unlikely, what would your three strongest arguments be?

## Week 3: Control

- Apply the win/continue/lose framework to a concrete deployment scenario (e.g., AI coding assistant, AI research assistant).
- Design a minimal control evaluation: what red-team attacks would you test? What blue-team defenses?
- Rank three control measures (monitoring, sandboxing, capability limitations) for a specific threat model. Justify your ordering.
- Identify a scenario where existing control measures would fail. What additional measures would you propose?

## Week 4: Governance / Strategy

- Assess the current AI governance landscape: who are the key actors, what are the main policy levers?
- Compare "race" vs. "slowdown" scenarios for AI development. What are the key decision points?
- Evaluate one of the proposed "plans" (Plans A-D for misalignment risk). What is its weakest assumption?
- Draft a 1-paragraph policy recommendation based on the readings this week. Who is the audience and what action should they take?

## Cross-week Synthesis

- How does your P(doom) estimate change from Week 1 to Week 4? What updated it most?
- Identify the single most important disagreement across the readings. Why does it matter?
- If you had 10 minutes to brief a policymaker on AI risk, what 3 points from the curriculum would you choose?
