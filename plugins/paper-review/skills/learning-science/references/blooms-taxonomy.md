# Bloom's Taxonomy — Question Stem Templates

Use these stems to generate questions at each cognitive level. Adapt to the specific paper content.

## Level 1: Remember
Recall facts and basic concepts.

- "What is [term/concept] as defined in the paper?"
- "List the [N] key [components/steps/findings]."
- "Who are the authors/researchers behind [X]?"
- "What dataset/method did the paper use for [X]?"
- "What year was [related work] published?"

## Level 2: Understand
Explain ideas or concepts.

- "Explain [concept] in your own words."
- "What is the difference between [X] and [Y] as described in the paper?"
- "Summarize the main argument of section [N]."
- "Why do the authors claim that [X]?"
- "What problem is the paper trying to solve?"
- "Paraphrase the paper's key finding about [X]."

## Level 3: Apply
Use information in new situations.

- "How would you use [method/framework] to [new situation]?"
- "If [variable] changed from [A] to [B], what would happen to [outcome]?"
- "Give an example of [concept] from a domain outside the paper."
- "How could [technique] be applied to [different problem]?"
- "Calculate/estimate [X] using the paper's formula/approach."

## Level 4: Analyze
Draw connections among ideas.

- "What assumptions does the paper make about [X]? Are they justified?"
- "Compare this paper's approach to [related work]. What are the trade-offs?"
- "What evidence supports the claim that [X]? Is it sufficient?"
- "Break down the argument for [conclusion] into its component claims."
- "What would happen if assumption [X] were violated?"
- "Identify a potential confound in the paper's experimental design."

## Level 5: Evaluate
Justify a stand or decision.

- "Do you agree with the authors' conclusion that [X]? Why or why not?"
- "Is the evidence sufficient to support the claim that [X]?"
- "What are the strongest and weakest parts of the paper's argument?"
- "How would you rate the paper's contribution to the field? Justify your rating."
- "If you were a reviewer, what would be your main criticism?"

## Level 6: Create
Produce new or original work.

- "How would you extend this work to address [limitation]?"
- "Design an experiment to test whether [claim] holds in [new context]."
- "Propose an alternative approach to solving [problem] that addresses [weakness]."
- "What research question does this paper leave unanswered? How would you investigate it?"
- "Combine ideas from this paper and [other work] to propose a new method."
